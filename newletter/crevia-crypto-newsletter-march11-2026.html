01 // Market Overview
The Setup: Relief Rally in a Deleveraged Market

The crypto market enters the second week of March 2026 caught between two opposing forces: persistent macro fear and early signs of structural bottoming. After Bitcoin fell back hard to the $65,000–$67,000 range on renewed geopolitical tensions and Trump's 15% global tariff announcement, the market staged a modest recovery — BTC is currently trading at approximately $69,967 as of March 11, holding above the critical $70,000 psychological level tested multiple times in recent weeks.

The total crypto market cap stands at $2.38 trillion, down significantly from cycle highs above $3.5T in late 2025. Bitcoin dominance remains elevated at 56.5%, well within "Bitcoin Season" territory on the CMC Altcoin Season Index (35/100), confirming that capital is not yet rotating into altcoins at any meaningful scale. A historic milestone was crossed on March 9: Bitcoin's 20 millionth coin was mined, leaving just ~1 million BTC remaining to be issued over coming decades — a supply scarcity event receiving surprisingly muted market attention given current sentiment.

The broad market narrative for March is best characterized as a deleveraged, range-bound reset with macro events — today's CPI release, March 18 FOMC decision, and the Clarity Act legislative process — serving as the key catalysts that will determine whether this is a consolidation floor or a continuation of the downtrend that began when BTC peaked near $126,000 in October 2025.

📍 Key Milestone: Bitcoin's 20 millionth coin mined March 9, 2026. Only ~1M BTC remain to be issued — ever. Annual supply issuance is now under 0.9% of total supply and falling with each halving. The next halving is projected for 2028.
